In a long essay about learning tools, the sentence that slowed the reader down was: Although the researchers who designed the study expected that students would remember more vocabulary when examples were shown in context, the results suggested that memory improved only when learners actively compared similar phrases and explained why one sounded more natural than another.

In a writing draft, the sentence the user wants to express in natural English is: 我想表达这个功能用起来很顺手，而且不会打断我正在阅读或写作的节奏。

The best moment is not when the user opens another app. It is the instant the meaning starts to blur.

A good language tool should stay close to the text, keep the learner in flow, and make the next action obvious.
